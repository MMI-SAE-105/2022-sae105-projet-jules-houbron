Social media addictions of new generations

While 90% of young people aged 16 to 24 say that social networks have an impact on their self-esteem, it is legitimate to wonder if the addiction of new generations to social networks is not harmful.

Collective addiction

Nearly 90% of 16-24 year olds use social networks, and for some of them, social networks represent a real addiction.

According to a study, social networks have an effect on happiness, self-esteem, loneliness and depression. The like has become a barometer of self-esteem, pushing young people to do sometimes stupid things for more likes.

Misuse

Social networks allow users easier and faster access to information, which also makes it easier to convey false information. Whether it is false information on public figures or on current topics, as we have seen on social networks in recent years during the pandemic, you should not believe everything you see. But these rumors can have even more serious consequences when, in the school setting for example, it is students who directly create rumors about other students creating cyberbullying.

Where once bullied students could find peace and comfort after school is over, they now face their bully directly even when they are home.

Behind a screen, people sometimes find it easier to criticize or insult other users, sometimes forgetting the consequences that their words can have on other users.

The addiction to social networks of new generations is a subject that I find important to deal with, since I consider myself to be addicted to social networks and I think that new generations must be educated on the use of these in order to prevent them from becoming too harmful.
