The mont Fuji

Above the clouds

Mount Fuji is a volcano with a height of 3,776 meters, the highest point in Japan. With its near-perfect symmetry, Mount Fuji was named by the country's first inhabitants, the Ainu, after their god of fire, Fuchi

Although it exudes calm and serenity, Mount Fuji is still considered an active volcano, and its almost symmetrical shape is attributed to a recent eruption, the so-called "Taiei Eruption", which began on December 16, 1707 and ended in 1708 around February 24th.

Source of artistic inspiration

Its size and beauty have made Mount Fuji an inspiration for many artists inJapan and around the world.
Painter and printmaker Katsushika Hokusai, also known as the "Old Painter", created a series of 36 views of Mount Fuji in the 1820s, including the famous Kanagawa wave.

The site was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2013 and is a "sacred site and source of artistic inspiration".

"Climbing Mount Fuji once is a wise man, and climbing twice is afool."

I really like this place because it exudes calm and serenity. It is a very spiritual place and is considered a sacred place in various religions including Shinto, Japan's main religion. Followers of Shinto believe that natural places are places called gods. The goddess Asama-sama is a god who lives on Mount Fuji. She represents the flowering of trees, and if she is respected, the volcano will not erupt.

Climbing Mount Fuji is a way of worshiping Asama-sama and purifying the soul. For Buddhists, Ascension is a metaphor for the path to enlightenment. The ascent begins in green nature and ends in the red highlands, which are made of volcanic rock and are believed to be the world of death.
